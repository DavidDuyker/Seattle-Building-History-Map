This is one of the earliest of Anhalt's apartment buildings to utilize Norman and medieval English details which established the design aesthetics for all of his subsequent apartments. Anhalt Arms was also constructed around the time that Fred Anhalt consolidated the development, design, construction, landscaping, marketing and management functions all into one firm. And so the Western Building and Leasing Company simply became the Anhalt Company. With this level of consolidation, the Anhalt Company was able to churn out many apartment buildings in just a few short years. Often, these apartments only took a mere 90 days between site preparation and occupancy. The time it took to complete Anhalt Arms here was actually just 43 days!  
  
As I've said, this apartment introduced many Norman design features which very soon became Anhalt trademarks. These features include a steeply pitched hipped roof with cedar shingles, utilization of clinker brick, expressive brick laying, square and round /polygonal turrets, and leaded glass windows all creatively combined with landscaping to compose a picturesque medieval setting. The creative brick laying on the Anhalt Arms primarily consists of darker clinker bricks which protrude slightly and are arranged in a haphazard diamond pattern. This diamond pattern is actually known as "diapering", and there are many other variations on this pattern. One surprising contribution from the protruding brick is that the accumulation of snow in the diapering pattern adds even more to the picturesqueness.  
  
In form, this one is similar to his other courtyard apartments except the lot size resulted in rather shorter wings and a shallower courtyard. This challenge is made up for by having some units with entrances accessed from outside the central courtyard. The otherwise symmetrical form of this C-shaped building is made more picturesque by giving the outside of the northern wing a square turret and the inside of the south wing a polygonal turret with a conical top.
