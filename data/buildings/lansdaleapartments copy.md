Source: Buildings of Seattle

This pie-shaped, four-story, 19-unit apartment building is among the oldest apartments in Capitol Hill and is a designated Seattle Landmark. Its curved façade that follows the contour of the gently sloped street gives the San Remo an unusual but charming presence in the neighborhood. Reading the façade vertically, it follows a gradient, stating with darker clinker brick at the base, buff brick in the middle, and light stucco at the top. Engaged Corinthian columns (with a very bizarre renovation if you look closely) enframe an arched recessed entry with Renaissance detailing. The shallow-hipped pseudo-roof with exposed rafter tails is really just a parapet masking the conventional flat roof behind it. The light color of the cladding, the red clay tile roof over the entry, and the Classical ornamentation vaguely evoke a Spanish or Italian Revival style.

The architect of the San Remo was William D Van Siclen from San Jose California, and he is likely partly responsible for popularizing the Spanish Eclectic style in Seattle.
