Hidden in plain sight on the bustling 12th Ave in Capitol Hill is this L-shaped Anhalt-designed courtyard apartment enshrouded in high hedges. Compared to some of Anhalt's other apartment buildings, the design of this one actually is relatively subdued. This apartment certainly features the trademark combination of Norman and Tudor elements with steeply-pitched hipped roofs and a picturesque assortment of dormers and balconies. All that being said, the design here lacks the whimsical panache as demonstrated at other apartments like Oak Manor. It is at least interesting to note that the central large gable adorning the inner corner of the courtyard is nearly identical in form to those in the Twin Gables courtyard apartment featured here several days ago. The layout of the interior behind this gable is also nearly identical between these two buildings, featuring a large living room with vaulted ceilings and exposed timbers and two curving staircase towards the back of the multi-level unit.  
  
Like a majority of Anhalt's other high-end apartment buildings, this one has subsequently been converted into condos.
