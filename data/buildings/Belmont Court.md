Almost right next door to Oak Manor, Belmont Court is situated on a similar triangular plot and has a similar irregular L-shaped plan, but it is somewhat larger. The larger scale of this building follows the trend of Anhalt's later apartments, which often necessitated shared entries for multiple units. This configuration change meant that the pseudo-townhome layout with each unit getting its own exterior entry had to be modified, but the result was anything but the steril apartments with anonymous corridors. Each of the shared entries feature extraordinarily detailed and unique doors ranging from rectangular, round arched, and Gothic pointed arched with solid wood paneling, stained glass, and decorative iron hinges.  
  
This single apartment building can almost be read as a collection of several smaller connected buildings due to complex massing and one of the party walls rising above the roofline, almost like parapet. Like Oak Manor, this apartment features some half-timbering, but in this case, the gaps between the timbers are filled with the more traditional white stucco rather than brick. But there is plenty of brickwork elsewhere on the façade with a heavy emphasis on clinker brick. Anhalt internationally utilized clinker brick due to its handmade appearance and the fact that it was considerably cheaper. Clinker bricks are unintentionally made when ordinary bricks are over-fired in the furnace and are typically considered "damaged goods" by most builders. Additionally, Anhalt recreated the texture of rustic brickwork by simply letting his bricklayers get "sloppy" and not worrying about mortar joints being slightly off. This design philosophy no doubt contributed to his apartments getting built at breakneck speed. But this apartment, which was completed after start of Great Depression, took a comparatively longer 6 months to construct.  
  
Another unusual feature of Belmont Court is its free-standing circle turret in the middle of the courtyard with a conical roof encasing a spiral staircase to an underground parking garage.
