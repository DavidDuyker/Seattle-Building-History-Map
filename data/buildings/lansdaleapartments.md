If I had to pick a favorite building shape, the flatiron would be toward the top of my list, especially when they are as narrow as this First Hill apartment building. I don't know who the architect was, but I can confidently say that its architectural style is a late example of the Queen Anne style that was used for larger scale masonry buildings. The taut red brick masonry enveloping the narrow wedge block is accented with a row of two story wooden polygonal bay windows of the sort commonly found on Queen Anne style homes. The roofline is demarcated by a simple dentiled cornice that follows the polygonal bays in an undulating rhythm.
