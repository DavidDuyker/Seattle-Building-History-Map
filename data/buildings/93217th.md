I particularly like this texturally rich Arts and Crafts home in Capitol Hill.  Starting at the first level, the use of dark clinker brick really helps to ground the house and its masonry appears to be of superior quality.  Note how over the segmental arched windows, the brickwork is laid in a “soldier course” pattern to rustically represent voussoirs and toward the center, these bricks are slightly raised to give the impression of keystones.  Towards the corners, the bricks are expressively corbelled outward to “support” the protruding beams at the transition to the second level.  This second level is given a course stuccoed treatment and the tight grid of the fenestration muntins serve to humanize the proportions of the home and emphasize a more humanistic scale (cozy vibes). Toward the gabled third level, small wooden brackets give the impression of jettying, even though the overhang is just a few inches.  The façade treatment of this level uses the same course stucco and is accented with vertical half-timbering and a Roman grill pattern beneath the central window, which has an even finer grid  muntin layout.  The overhanging roof is supported by Craftsman brackets and the bargeboard tails have a unique pattern.
