Here it is, Anhalt's magnum opus, the Capitol Hill castlesque apartment which is his largest and most whimsical. Anhalt himself described it as "the best building I ever built."  
  
And as large as this apartment is, rising to 4 stories in some parts, it still retains the human scale of his other courtyard apartments. In plan, this apartment is F-shaped, with a large central courtyard facing Roy and a smaller corner courtyard towards Federal Ave.  
  
Even though both this apartment and its companion across the street were both under construction at the same time in 1928, this one was not complete until 1930. This is not too surprising, considering the magnificence and scale of the project in combination with the bad timing of the market crash.  
  
This massive courtyard apartment features not one, but two stair towers, as well as a smaller freestanding turret, all with conical roofs. The grandest of these towers includes cast stone trim and pointed arch windows to give it a truly Gothic appearance. These towers also featured among the earliest instances of freestanding staircases in Seattle.  
  
Another superlative this masterpiece features is Seattle's first ever underground parking garage in an apartment building. And impressively, this auto-centric amenity does not detract at all from the picturesque design of the apartment as was the case with much modern residential architecture. But rather, the garage entry through a ponted arch in the clinker brick façade more resembles the portcullis of a castle. The construction of this underground 24-car garage was also apparently a great engineering feat in its day. Both the building foundation and garage slab were poured in a single day, and was the biggest concrete pour in Seattle at the time. This concrete pour unfortunately coincided with rain later in the day, but once it started, it couldn't end until they were done. But Anhalt helped make this task less arduous by providing a case of whisky for his crew.
