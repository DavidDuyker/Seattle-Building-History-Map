This apartment building can almost be considered a smaller version of the iconic 1005 E Roy. The expanse of clinker brick is broken up with a half-timbered projection along the far wall of the 3rd floor in both apartments. And the circular stair turret nestled in one of the courtyard corners is nearly identical in both apartments, featuring the same tripartite pointed arch windows and cast stone ornamentation, like the Gothic hood molding over the every way. The massing of this building is much more simplified, with a U-shaped plan and single rectangular courtyard.  
  
One aspect that unfortunately sets this Anhalt apartment building apart from the rest is the lack of original interior design features. That is because by the 1960s, most of the interior was gutted to make room for Group Health’s administrative offices. This building then sat vacant for about 4 years before it was acquired by Trinity Real Estate for $2.7 million to be renovated and converted back into apartments. Fortunately, most of the exterior was kept intact, so that today this building still retains the signature Anhalt look.
