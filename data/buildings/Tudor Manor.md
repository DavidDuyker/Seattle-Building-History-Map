Here's another courtyard apartment with strong Tudor tendencies that was designed by Anhalt. Or was it?  
  
The building certainly looks like an Anhalt apartment with its form, style, and attention to detail. Additionally, the year it was completed is at the peak of Anhalt's prolific apartment output and the location is at the heart where most of his apartments were built, with this one just across the street from Anhalt Arms. So naturally, it is very easy to believe that this is yet another of Anhalt's iconic designs. Tudor Manor is in fact quite frequently referenced in lists of apartments designed by Anhalt, including the NRHP nomination form for Anhalt's Quinita Apartments. However, among the Seattle architecture books I have perused which discuss the built works of Anhalt, this apartment does not receive the slightest mention. So is this an oversight of architectural historians, or is everyone else blindly giving Anhalt credit for something he had no hand in?  
  
A closer look at the design of Tudor Manor may be revealing. One aspect that immediately stands out is how this courtyard is much deeper than any of Anhalt's other courtyard apartments, but this is simply due to the size and shape of the lot. The predominantly Tudor, rather than Norman design is atypical for an Anhalt design, but not unheard of as seen at Twin Gables. However, the design of the façade at the rear of the courtyard features some un-Anhalt Jacobethan tendencies with the parapets, hood molding over the windows, and stone enframed doorway. The stylization of the half-timbering and bargeboards of the gable ends appear somewhat different from Anhalt's other works, but this could just be an example of his unique touch given to each building. But the half-timbering of the central gable does feature the inset brickwork which is a trademark in some of Anhalt's works. The brickwork elsewhere is otherwise suspiciously tame, but some clinker bricks pop up here and there. The roof does not feature Anhalt's in-house cedar shingles, but these could have been replaced at some point as has occurred at other apartments of his.
