Hidden behind a tall hedge in the Montlake neighborhood, this classy brick Tudor cottage was once apparently the most talked about home in Seattle when it was being built. This home was built by the Northwest Brick and Tile Association as a model home in an effort to demonstrate to the city the desirability, durability, low maintenance, and affordability of a brick home when compared to a typical framed home. The home went up surprisingly quickly in just 12 weeks and every detail of the construction exhaustively covered in weekly articles appearing in the Seattle Times. This effort would appear to be a resounding success, judging by the profuse number of brick Tudor homes in Montlake and beyond.

The design of this Tudor Revival house simply consists of a steep side gable roof with a similarly steep gable over the front off-center entrance and balanced by a shed dormer with four square casement windows of diamond-paned leaded glass. The roof is clad with flat shingle-style red tile and the home is clad in patterned red brick veneer. The front gable brick masonry is even more elaborate with a distinct diapering pattern in the contrasting brick tones.
