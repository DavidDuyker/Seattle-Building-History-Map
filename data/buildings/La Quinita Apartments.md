But who is Anhalt?  
Frederick William Anhalt was one of the few well-known architects in Seattle from the early 20th century who was not professionally trained, but came out of the building trade. He is best known for designing high-quality courtyard apartments, typically in the Tudor and Norman Revival styles. In the mid 1920s, he established a development organization called Western Building and Leasing Company. Early on, WBLC hired architects and contractors to design and build their apartments, but eventually, Anhalt brought all these functions in-house.  
  
So who actually designed La Quinita? This can be somewhat convoluted, but in a nutshell, it can be said this apartment was designed by Frederick Anhalt, while William H. Whiteley, who prepared the permit and construction drawings, is listed as the official architect. La Quinita is both Anhalt's earliest courtyard apartment and the first to emphasize landscape design, but unlike his later Norman styled apartments, this one was designed a in Mediterranean Revival / Spanish Ecclectic style as can be seen in the low pitched tile clad hipped roofs and painted stucco exterior walls.  
  
Not much of this two-story U-shaped building can be seen from the street besides its exterior walls due to the extensive landscaping of the courtyard. The most noticeable feature as seen from the street is the pair of round arched raised panels on the ends of the wings. These feature narrow angled stones set into the stucco imitating voussoirs of an arch. The raised panels consist of molded stucco with multicolored marbled tiles.
