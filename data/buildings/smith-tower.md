## Overview

When it opened in **1914**, Smith Tower was the tallest building west of the Mississippi and a symbol of Seattle’s growth after the Klondike gold rush.

## Architecture

The tower blends neoclassical styling with a distinctive pyramid crown and Chinese room observatory, which still offers views over Pioneer Square and Elliott Bay.
